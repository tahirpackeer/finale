BENTOTA TURTLE HATCHERY

BENTOTA HATCHERY

One of the Sri Lankan turtle hatcheries that has had a significant impact on the upbringing of sea turtles is the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery. As we all are aware, poachers and other external factors pose a threat to sea turtles in Sri Lanka and across the world. It's also a location that informs tourists about the various sea turtles and the challenges they encounter at sea. The hatchery was established about 40 years ago and is still in operation today.

Six of the seven species of sea turtles are now in risk of extinction. The majority of it results from the exploitation of natural resources by humans. Thankfully, this hatchery has accepted responsibility for their protection. Beginning with watching over the nesting mother turtles, they next transport their eggs to the safe environment of the hatchery and, on the day of birth, release the young turtles into the sea. If there are problems, they tend to them and only release them when they are well enough to enter the huge ocean.

All five of the country's species may be found making their nests in Kosgoda, one of the relatively few hatcheries. They are the Hawksbill, Leatherback, Green, and Olive Ridley turtles.

How does the hatchery operate?

People go out and gather turtle eggs from the sand in the coastal areas, which they then sell to the hatchery for money. They wait till the eggs have hatched before burying these eggs once more in the sand. The hatchlings are then housed in a tank for a few days until they are prepared to be released into the sea, where they are fed and cared for.
